## Why Meta Descriptions Still Matter

In the world of SEO, Google's algorithms are constantly changing. However, one thing remains constant: the importance of the **Meta Description**. While Google has stated it's not a *direct* ranking factor, it affects your **Click-Through Rate (CTR)**, which *is* a massive ranking factor.

### The Psychology of a Click

When a user searches for something, the meta description is your "elevator pitch." You have roughly 160 characters to convince them that your page holds the answer to their query.

**A Good Meta Description:**
* **Includes the Keyword:** Google often bolds these, drawing the eye.
* **Solves a Problem:** "Learn how to fix X..."
* **Has a Call to Action:** "Read more," "Discover," "Get started."

### Best Practices Checklist
1.  Keep it under 155-160 characters.
2.  Make it unique for every page (No duplicates!).
3.  Match the search intent (Informational vs. Transactional).
4.  Use active voice.
